04

Motivation Switches

Autonomy, mastery, and purpose are the three levers of sustained drive. Set them before setting goals.

Autonomy
The ability to direct your own work, time, and methods. Without it, motivation collapses into compliance.
I choose to direct my life by...
Mastery
The drive to get better at something that matters. Mastery requires both challenge and feedback.
I want to regularly get better at...
Purpose
The sense that what you do serves something larger than yourself. Purpose is not found — it is chosen.
The work that matters most to me is... because...

08

Anti-Plan

You can't control the year. You can control how you move through it. Most plans fail because they try to predict the future. An Anti-Plan separates what you can shape from what you can't.

What I cannot control
Other people. External events. Timing. Luck. Markets. Health shocks.
What I can control
Actions. Habits. Attention. Boundaries. Systems. Responses.
My Anchor Principles for 2026
One or two sentences describing how you will respond when the year doesn't go as planned. Return to this when things break.

The Endurance Test

Jerry Seinfeld: the secret to success is finding the torture you're willing to endure. Progress comes from choosing the discomfort you're willing to repeat.

What deep discomfort am I willing to endure regularly to get great at something?
Be specific. This must be something you can do weekly or daily. Vague commitment = zero commitment.
Three skills that have already rewarded long-term effort
Evidence that you can endure and improve. This is your proof of concept.
